Categorical Probability and Polyhedral Fun

$S$-vertex simplices parameterize (finite-support) distributions $P(s)$ over $S$ possibilities. To generalize, an affine map to an $S$-vertex simplex from a $K$-vertex simplex gives the same data as a conditional (finite-support) distribution $P(s;k)$. The unconditional case we recover by setting $K=1$, the terminal simplex. Thus, we may interpret distributional data as affine maps between simplices.

So, what happens if we generalize simplices to all convex sets? It turns out the resulting category has (co)limits, tensor, and hom, and we may interpret these concepts probabilistically, too! In fact, given slightly restrictive re-definitions of statistical concepts, we may also show Basu's and Bahadur's theorems.

For more details, please see my Problyhedra talk at the MIT Categories seminar!
