Rocks Ab Initio

Notes on Geology by Samuel Tenka

Hello

Look around your apartment, and you'll see many rocks. From chalk's porous limestone to the smooth slate of blackboards, rocks --- that is, solid mineral aggregates --- abound. Now scan beyond your window: notice a passerby slipping on compacted snow; observe the machinations of an empire built on the energy of coal; imagine the obsidian arrowheads veiled from view by grass and soil and millenia. Rocks are Nature's building blocks.

But rocks are not static: in their constant flows and fluxes they are the very scroll on which Nature writes her history. Hold a rock, and feel the weight of its age, the wisdom of its dimpled skin. Its molecules may well have witnessed the birth of life itself. In their eternal dance of deposition and erosion, those molecules endure the cruel pressures of inner earth, rising only as tortured tectonic plates screech and wrestle and belch their innards out. As exiles, they drift from dune to dune across ancient deserts. Some even dissolve in animal stomachs only to reincarnate as the sheen of a beautiful shell. Geology, then, distinguishes itself in the pace and subtle violence of the processes it studies.

These notes tour the many facets of rocks and their history. Assuming no background, we build from the basics: quantum field theory to explain the material properties of rock, cosmology to describe the origins of rock, neo darwinian theory to understand the interactions of rock and life, and fluid dynamics to model the climate that so affects surface geology. From here, we briefly describe a diverse set of processes such as the saltation of sand and the life cycle of hoodoos. We finally survey two major applications of rocks: the construction of Turing-complete machines from silicon and rock-based survival skills.

All said, geology is a central science. If Aristotle's earth tended to sink below water, air, and fire, so too does geology tend to motivate the more applied sciences of physics, chemistry, and biology. But to call geology impractical is to err. While our technical preparations may petrify the uninitiated, just think, dear reader, whenever you waver, of the dominion of human above rock in that grandest of engineering triumphs: Khufu's pyramid. Oriented thus with curiosity and pluck, let us climb our own pyramid. We begin at the beginning: with field theory.

Nature is Random

Though the soul of science lies in experiment, it turns out that an experiment's initial conditions do not determine its outcome. Indeed, as scientists, we might work with pure states, each of which encodes the state of the universe via a maximal amount of knowledge about future experiments.

If $a$ and $b$ are pure states and $E$ is an experiment, let us write $P(a|E|b)$ for the probability of arriving at $a$ via $E$ from $b$.

Quantum physics postulates that the pure states correspond to the unit-norm vectors in a complex Hilbert space and that each experiment can be encoded in a linear map such that $P(a|E|b) = \left| \langle a | E(b)\rangle \right|^2$ whenever $a$ is a possible outcome of $E$. In fact, the experimental outcomes form an orthonormal basis for the whole Hilbert space. Then a sequence of experiments with final result $a$, intermediate result $b$, and initial result $c$ has outcome probability $P(a|E|b|F|c) = \left| \langle a|E(b)\rangle \langle b|F(c)\rangle \right|^2$ However, if we don't measure the intermediate result, we sum over possible intermediate states inside the norm: $$P(a|EF|c) = \left|\sum_b \langle a|E(b)\rangle \langle b|F(c) \rangle \right|^2 = \left|\langle a|EF(c)\right|^2$$ Those are the rules of quantum physics.

The inner products are called amplitudes.

What does it mean to live? What is life? These are some of the central questions of biology, and hence by extension geology. Toward a definition, we note these characteristics of life as we intuitively understand it:

(A) Life reproduces (potentially with mutation) and hence undergoes natural selection.

(B) The information that reproduces is encoded chemically.
