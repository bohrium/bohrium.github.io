Module Theory

Notes by Sam Tenka based on courses by Andrew Snowden and Mel Hochster

Modules (over a ring) generalize vector spaces (over a field). Thus, the module theory generalizes linear algebra. In allowing the underlying ring to lack division, new properties emerge. For instance, not every module is free. Moreover, whenever $0\to A\to B\to C\to 0$ is exact between vector spaces, then $C\cong B/A$ (naturally) and $B\cong A\oplus C$ (unnaturally). However, between modules, the latter conclusion need not hold. For instance, take modules over the integers $\ZZ$; then $0\to 2\ZZ\to \ZZ\to \ZZ/2\ZZ\to 0$. But $\ZZ\ncong 2\ZZ \oplus\ZZ/2\ZZ$!
